Critical

Zero presence in AI-powered search

0 Google AI Overview appearances. No llms.txt file. As ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Mode answer queries like "best plumber North Vancouver," businesses with GEO infrastructure will be cited first. SMR is entirely invisible in this growing channel today.

High

Create llms.txt file (30-minute fix)

The llms.txt file is the "robots.txt for AI." It tells AI crawlers what the site is about, which pages matter, and what services are offered. A missing llms.txt file is a significant missed opportunity for AI citation.

High

Upgrade Organization schema to LocalBusiness/Plumber type

The site uses a generic Organization schema but is missing Plumber schema (a LocalBusiness subtype). This schema should include: service area (areaServed), all service types, geo coordinates, opening hours, and payment methods. This directly improves AI Overview and Google Local Pack citations.

Medium

25% dynamic rendering blocks AI crawlers

AI search crawlers don't execute JavaScript. Approximately 25% of the site's content is injected by JS (likely Elementor sliders and lazy-loaded sections) and is invisible to AI systems. Key content — services, locations, contact details — must be in the initial HTML response.

Medium

Add FAQPage schema to all service pages

AI search specifically extracts FAQ-formatted content for citation. Adding FAQ sections (with FAQPage schema) to each service page dramatically increases the probability of being cited in AI answers. Each FAQ answer should be 2–4 sentences and directly answer a real customer question.

Critical

Business address missing from website

The website does not display the business address anywhere, breaking NAP (Name, Address, Phone) consistency — a core local ranking signal. The Google Business Profile address is: 1081 Roosevelt Crescent, North Vancouver, BC V7P 1M4. This must appear in the footer on every page, formatted identically to the GBP listing.

Critical

No LocalBusiness schema

Despite having JSON-LD on the site, there is no LocalBusiness or Plumber schema type. This is the most important structured data signal for Local Pack rankings. The schema must include: geo coordinates, service area by city, opening hours, and all service types offered.

High

No location-specific service pages exist

No dedicated pages exist for key sub-markets. Pages targeting "plumber [neighbourhood]" rank more easily than generic city-wide terms because they face less competition. Priority pages to create: North Vancouver, West Vancouver, Lonsdale, Lynn Valley / Deep Cove, Edgemont Village, Burnaby, Strata Plumbing North Vancouver.

Medium

109 reviews is strong — 300+ would be dominant

Competitors Milani and Pioneer have 500–2,000+ reviews. A systematic post-job SMS review request (sent within 2 hours of job completion, with a direct GBP link) could realistically add 3–5 reviews per week. Tools: NiceJob, Broadly, or a Zapier-automated SMS workflow.

Medium

GBP Posts, Services menu, and Q&A underutilized

Weekly GBP Posts signal business activity to Google. The Services menu should list every service with descriptions and price ranges where possible. Proactively add and answer 10+ Q&A entries in GBP. Add 50+ geotagged job-site photos to the GBP listing.

High

Mobile PageSpeed 39/100 is costing emergency leads

70–80% of emergency plumbing searches happen on mobile. A 15.7s Time to Interactive means potential customers bounce before the page loads. Primary causes: 2.9s server response time, 58 JS resources, 1.35MB total JavaScript weight, images only 4% compressed.

Upgrade hosting to Kinsta or WP Engine, fully configure WP Rocket (Critical CSS, defer JS), audit and eliminate unused plugins, enable WebP image conversion.
Medium

Missing SPF and DMARC DNS records

No SPF or DMARC records found for the email domain. These records prevent domain spoofing and significantly improve email deliverability for quote follow-ups and booking confirmations. Both records can be added in under 30 minutes via the DNS management panel.

Low

iFrames and inline styles adding overhead

iFrames (likely the Google Maps embed) add significant load time and complicate mobile rendering. Replace with a static map image linking to Google Maps. Elementor-generated inline styles inflate HTML size and degrade page maintainability.

Low

Plain text email address exposed

The email address displayed in plain text on the website is vulnerable to spam scraper bots. Replace with a contact form or a CSS-obfuscated email display method.

High

Service pages are too thin to rank competitively

Current service pages appear to be 200–400 words. Competitive pages for terms like "heat pump installation north vancouver" need 800–1,200 words covering: what's involved, why BC's climate suits heat pumps, available rebate programs, the installation process, typical costs, and FAQs.

Content priority: Heat Pumps › Gas Fitting › Emergency Plumbing › HVAC/AC › Strata/Commercial.
High

FortisBC/BC Hydro rebate content is a major untapped opportunity

BC residents actively research heat pump rebates (BC Hydro up to $3,000, FortisBC up to $6,000 combined). A dedicated page — "Heat Pump Rebates in BC: What North Shore Homeowners Need to Know" — would capture high-intent research traffic and be highly cited by AI search systems. As a FortisBC registered contractor, SMR owns this topic locally.

Medium

Blog content will compound over 12–18 months

Target 1–2 posts per month on highly specific, answerable questions. Not volume — precision. Priority topics: "Why does my furnace short-cycle in North Vancouver winters?", "How to choose between a heat pump and furnace in BC", "What to do if you smell gas in your home: a North Vancouver guide", "Complete guide to strata plumbing responsibilities in BC", "Hot water tank vs tankless: what's right for your North Shore home?"

Low

Portfolio section is an underutilized SEO asset

Each portfolio entry should include: project type, scope, specific technologies installed (e.g., "Mitsubishi Zuba-Central heat pumps in a 12-unit North Vancouver strata building"), and neighbourhood-level location. This transforms a passive photo gallery into indexed, keyword-rich content.
